It is easy to feel frightened when we contemplate the future of our country my fellow Americans. We read the news. It is not good. We go to the store. Things cost a lot of money. We witness weather shifting corporations struggling and our communities crumbling. It is tempting, as we consider the challenges in front of us, to believe that our best days are over.

I’m not here to discuss what’s going wrong. And I’m here to tell you what we’re going to do about it. The future is what we are now building step by step.

Think about what our country will look like in a decade. Imagine a country where when someone gets sick it doesn’t mean their family will go bankrupt trying to pay the bills. We will harness the energy of the wind that blows across our lands and the sun that beats down on our homes. There will be tons of good-paying jobs that will open up because of this. Our children will be — in class reading and learning, not rehearsing how to respond if a gunman shows up. They will be dreaming of all the things they can do when they are older
