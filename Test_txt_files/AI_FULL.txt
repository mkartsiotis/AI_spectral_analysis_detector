My fellow Americans, when we look down the road at the future of this country, it’s easy to feel a sense of dread. We see the headlines. We feel the squeeze at the grocery store. We watch our climate change, our institutions stumble, and our communities fracture. It is easy to look at the mounting challenges and decide that our best days are permanently behind us.

But I am not standing here today to tell you what is broken. I am here to talk about what we are going to build. The future isn't some distant destination that just happens to us; it is a house we are constructing right now, brick by brick, with our own two hands.

Imagine a decade from now. Imagine a country where a medical diagnosis doesn't mean financial ruin for a working family. Where our energy comes from the wind sweeping across our plains and the sun beating down on our rooftops, creating millions of good-paying, union jobs in the process. Imagine a society where our children aren't practicing lockdown drills in their classrooms, but are instead solely focused on reading, creating, and dreaming about their own boundless potential.

That future is not a fantasy.
