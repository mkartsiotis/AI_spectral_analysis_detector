Whenever we outlook at the future of this nation, my fellow Americans, it is easy to get a sense of dread. We see the headlines. We experience the squeeze in the grocery store. We are spectators of our weather shifting, our organizations tripping and our societies breaking apart. It is not hard to examine the straddling issues and determine that our best days have been erased forever.

But I do not stand before you to-day to tell you what is broken. I have come to discuss what we are going to build. The future is not a place to which we are going to arrive, it is a home we are building now, brick by brick, two-hand construction.

Imagine a decade from now. Think of a nation where a medical diagnosis does not equal the death of a working family financially. Where we will generate energy on the wind blowing over our plains and the sun blazing on our roof tops, and in the process, generate millions of good paying, union jobs. We all live in a society where our children are not doing lockdown drills in their classrooms, but rather they are simply occupying themselves with reading, creating, and fantasizing about their own limitless potential.

That future is not a fantasy.
