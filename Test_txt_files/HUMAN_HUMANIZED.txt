For many, the rise in violence on television is directly responsible for the recent rise in violence and cruelty within our communities, while others believe that television violence has no negative effect on peoples' behaviour. This is an emotional and controversial topic, so both sides of the argument should be given equal consideration. 

For those that advocate for television shows containing violence, their argument is based on the belief that television programmes containing violence will not affect society and that a majority of people are able to distinguish between fact and fiction. Therefore, they will not imitate violent or undesirable behaviour as portrayed on the screen; in addition they know that problem resolution will always lead to non-violent methods. Additionally, in today's society, entertainment is subject to an individual's desire to follow their individual freedom to select entertainment; therefore restricting this individual right of access to certain types of entertainment is a violation of that individual's right to choose entertainment. Additionally, while children and teenagers are likely influenced more by violence on television than most adults, there is ample television rating systems and the requirement for parents to monitor their child’s television viewing behaviour.
